Discover Sorsogon

Experience the natural wonders and cultural treasures of our beautiful province

Sorsogon, the southernmost province of Luzon, is a treasure trove of natural wonders and cultural riches, waiting to be discovered. The province boasts an array of breathtaking attractions, including pristine beaches and islands with crystal-clear waters and powdery white sand, majestic volcanoes that offer thrilling hiking and trekking experiences, wildlife encounters that allow visitors to get up close and personal with the region's unique fauna, culturally significant landmarks that showcase the province's rich history and heritage, unique experiences that can't be found anywhere else, such as exploring the province's scenic countryside or trying local delicacies.

Our carefully curated selection of attractions showcases the very best of Sorsogon, with each location chosen for its natural beauty, cultural significance, or unforgettable experiences. Whether you're a nature lover, a culture vulture, or an adventure-seeker, Sorsogon has something for everyone.

Top Destinations

Must-visit natural wonders in Sorsogon

Donsol Whale Shark Interaction

Donsol, Sorsogon

Experience swimming with the gentle giants of the sea in their natural habitat. Donsol is world-renowned for its sustainable whale shark tourism, where these magnificent creatures, locally known as "butanding," can be observed without feeding or disturbing them.

The best time to visit is from November to June, with peak sightings from February to April. Beyond whale sharks, Donsol also offers firefly watching tours along the Ogod River and beautiful beaches for relaxation.

Bulusan Volcano Natural Park

Bulusan, Sorsogon

Often called the "Switzerland of the Orient" for its cool climate and lush landscapes, Bulusan Volcano Natural Park is a protected area showcasing the majestic Bulusan Volcano and its surrounding forests, lakes, and hot springs.

The centerpiece is Bulusan Lake, a serene crater lake surrounded by dense rainforest. Visitors can enjoy kayaking on the lake, trekking through the forest trails, relaxing in Masacrot Hot Springs, and birdwatching in this natural sanctuary.

Learn More

Matnog Pink Beaches

Matnog, Sorsogon

Located at the southernmost tip of Luzon, Matnog is home to some of the most stunning beaches and islands in the Philippines. The area is famous for its pink beaches, crystal-clear waters, and marine sanctuaries teeming with life.

Subic Beach and Tikling Island feature pinkish sand due to crushed red corals mixing with white sand. Island hopping tours also include visits to Juag Lagoon Marine Sanctuary, where you can swim among various fish species, and the mysterious Calintaan Cave.

Learn More

Barcelona Church and Ruins

Barcelona, Sorsogon

Step back in time at the historic Barcelona Church, a Spanish colonial-era structure built in the 1800s. This church, dedicated to St. Joseph, features impressive stone architecture and is considered one of the most beautiful churches in the Bicol region.

Nearby are the ruins of an old Spanish complex, offering a glimpse into the province's colonial past. The site provides excellent photo opportunities and a peaceful atmosphere for those interested in history and architecture.

Learn More

Rizal Beach

Gubat, Sorsogon

Rizal Beach in Gubat is becoming increasingly popular among surfers and beach lovers. With its long stretch of gray sand, consistent waves, and laid-back atmosphere, it's the perfect spot for both beginners and experienced surfers.

The beach offers stunning sunrise views and is less crowded than many other tourist destinations, allowing for a more peaceful experience. Local surf instructors are available for lessons, and nearby accommodations range from budget-friendly to mid-range options.

Vibrant Festivals

Experience the colorful cultural celebrations of Sorsogon

October

Kasanggayahan Festival

A week-long celebration commemorating the founding of Sorsogon as a province. The festival features street dancing, trade fairs, cultural shows, and various competitions that showcase the province's rich heritage.

June

Pili Festival

Celebrated in Sorsogon City, this festival honors the pili nut, one of the province's most important agricultural products. Activities include cooking competitions, cultural presentations, and a street parade featuring pili-themed floats.

May

Parau Festival

Held in Gubat, this festival celebrates the town's fishing industry with a fluvial parade of decorated boats, fishing competitions, and seafood feasts. The highlight is the street dancing competition depicting fishing activities.

Local Delicacies

Savor the unique flavors of Sorsogon cuisine

Spicy

Bicol Express

A signature Bicolano dish made with pork, chili peppers, coconut milk, shrimp paste, and various spices. Sorsogon's version is known for its perfect balance of creaminess and heat.

Traditional

Laing

Dried taro leaves cooked in coconut milk with chili peppers, meat, and seafood. This creamy, spicy dish is a staple in Bicolano cuisine and is often served with steamed rice.

Sweet

Pili Nut Delicacies

Various sweet treats made from the native pili nut, including mazapan de pili (pili marzipan), pili tarts, and candied pili nuts. These delicacies showcase the versatility of this nutritious nut.

Local Products

Bring home authentic souvenirs from Sorsogon

Handicraft

Abaca Products

Handcrafted items made from abaca fiber, including bags, placemats, hats, and home decor. Sorsogon is known for its high-quality abaca, often called Manila hemp, which is prized for its strength and versatility.

Wellness

Pili Oil Products

Natural skincare and wellness products made from pili oil, which is extracted from pili nuts. These include soaps, lotions, massage oils, and lip balms known for their moisturizing and anti-aging properties.

Traditional

Banig Mats

Intricately woven mats made from pandan or buri palm leaves. These colorful mats feature traditional patterns and designs unique to the region and are used as sleeping mats, wall decorations, or floor coverings.

More Destinations

Discover these hidden gems across Sorsogon

Island

Paguriran Island

A small island in Bacon, Sorsogon City, featuring a stunning lagoon surrounded by limestone formations. During low tide, visitors can walk to the island and swim in its crystal-clear waters.

Eco-Tourism

Buhatan River Cruise

Experience a relaxing boat ride along the Buhatan River in Sorsogon City, surrounded by lush mangroves and diverse wildlife. The cruise includes cultural presentations and a taste of local delicacies.

Nature

Prieto Diaz Mangrove Forest

One of the most well-preserved mangrove forests in the Philippines, featuring a boardwalk that allows visitors to explore the ecosystem. It's a haven for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts.
